# BTQ Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the BTQ community a harassment-free experience for everyone.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, and healthy community focused on advancing quantum-resistant cryptocurrency technology.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Technical Focus**: Keep discussions centered on technical merit and project goals
- **Respectful Communication**: Use welcoming and inclusive language
- **Constructive Feedback**: Provide specific, actionable feedback on code and ideas
- **Collaborative Spirit**: Help others learn and contribute effectively
- **Professional Conduct**: Maintain professionalism in all interactions
- **Evidence-Based**: Support arguments with code, benchmarks, or research
- **Patient Teaching**: Help newcomers understand complex concepts
- **Graceful Disagreement**: Disagree with ideas while respecting people

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- **Personal Attacks**: Insulting, derogatory comments, or personal attacks
- **Harassment**: Public or private harassment, trolling, or intimidation
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory language or behavior
- **Spam/Off-Topic**: Excessive off-topic discussion or promotional content
- **Doxxing**: Publishing private information without consent
- **Threats**: Threats of violence or harm
- **Disruption**: Deliberately derailing technical discussions
- **Bad Faith**: Arguing in bad faith or refusing to engage constructively

### Communication Standards

#### GitHub (Issues, PRs, Discussions)

- **Stay On Topic**: Keep discussions relevant to the issue or PR
- **Be Specific**: Provide clear, actionable feedback with examples
- **Cite Sources**: Link to relevant code, documentation, or research
- **Acknowledge Others**: Credit contributors and reviewers appropriately
- **Professional Tone**: Maintain professional language even during disagreements

#### Telegram/IRC/Matrix

- **Announcement Focus**: Keep announcements channel for official communications only
- **Redirect Technical**: Direct technical discussions back to GitHub
- **Help Others**: Assist community members with basic questions
- **No Support DMs**: Encourage public discussion for transparency

## Enforcement

### Responsibilities

Community leaders and moderators are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

### Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories (issues, PRs, discussions, wikis)
- Telegram channels and groups
- IRC/Matrix channels
- Mailing lists
- Social media accounts
- In-person events and meetups
- Any other BTQ-related communication

### Enforcement Guidelines

#### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

#### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

#### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

#### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Reporting Violations

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to:

- **Primary Contact**: oscar@btq.org or barney@btq.org
- **GitHub**: Use the "Report content" feature on GitHub
- **Telegram**: Contact administrators directly

### What to Include

- Description of the incident
- Where it occurred (GitHub, Telegram, etc.)
- Screenshots or links if applicable
- Any relevant context
- Your contact information for follow-up

### Response Process

1. **Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: Review evidence and gather additional information
3. **Decision**: Determine appropriate response based on guidelines
4. **Action**: Implement consequences and communicate to relevant parties
5. **Follow-up**: Monitor for compliance and effectiveness

### Confidentiality

- Reports will be handled confidentially
- Only necessary personnel will be involved
- Reporter identity protected unless permission given
- Public disclosure only if required for transparency

## Appeals Process

### Right to Appeal

Any person subject to enforcement action may appeal the decision by:

1. **Submitting Appeal**: Email conduct@btq.org within 30 days
2. **Provide Rationale**: Explain why the decision should be reconsidered
3. **Review Process**: Independent review by uninvolved moderators
4. **Final Decision**: Communicated within 14 days

### Appeal Criteria

Appeals will be considered based on:
- New evidence not available during initial investigation
- Procedural errors in the enforcement process
- Disproportionate response to the violation
- Changed circumstances or demonstrated improvement

## Special Considerations

### Technical Disagreements

Technical disagreements are not Code of Conduct violations. The appropriate response to technical disagreements is:

1. **Present Evidence**: Provide code, benchmarks, or research
2. **Seek Expert Opinion**: Involve domain experts in the discussion
3. **Build Consensus**: Work toward rough consensus through discussion
4. **Accept Decisions**: Respect final decisions by maintainers

### Security-Sensitive Discussions

- **Embargo Respect**: Honor responsible disclosure timelines
- **No Public Speculation**: Avoid public discussion of unconfirmed vulnerabilities
- **Professional Handling**: Maintain professionalism during security incidents

## Community Values

### Technical Excellence

- **Merit-Based**: Decisions based on technical merit and evidence
- **Quality Focus**: Prioritize correctness and security over speed
- **Continuous Learning**: Encourage learning and knowledge sharing
- **Innovation**: Support responsible innovation in post-quantum cryptography

### Sustainability

- **Long-term Thinking**: Consider long-term impact of decisions
- **Maintainer Health**: Prevent burnout and support work-life balance
- **Knowledge Transfer**: Document processes and share expertise
- **Community Growth**: Nurture new contributors and leaders
